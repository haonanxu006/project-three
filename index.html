Popular Apple Varieties in the US

Data from the 2007 Agricultural Resource Management Survey show that the top five apple varieties-Red Delicious, Golden Delicious, Gala, Fuji, and Granny Smith-were the same in conventional and organic production, although the order differed between the two production systems. These five varieties accounted for over two-thirds of apple production in the surveyed States. Red Delicious, Gala, and Fuji are primarily fresh-market apples, while Golden Delicious and Granny Smith are "dual-purpose" apples that can be used in either fresh or processing markets.

After a few years of caring, we now have some fruits!

Most apple varieties have flowers that contain male and female parts and so are self-pollinating. These trees will set fruit without cross-pollination. However, there are some varieties that are self-infertile and require a pollenizer, such as bees.

An established apple tree requires only infrequent watering but be sure to water all trees during prolonged dry periods.

Newly planted apple trees require moderate watering weekly. Set the water on low and allow it to seep into the soil.

Before apples seeds germinate, they need to be exposed to cool, moist conditions, which can be achieved by planting them outdoor in fall. Then, plant them 1/2 inch deep.
